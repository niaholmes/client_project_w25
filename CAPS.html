CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services)

At the University of Michigan, your mental health and well-being are a top priority. Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) offers a variety of support resources to help you navigate the challenges of college life. Our dedicated team is here to ensure you feel supported, understood, and empowered.

What is CAPS?

CAPS is the primary mental health service for U-M students, providing a range of confidential services designed to support your emotional and psychological well-being during your time at university. We are committed to fostering a safe and inclusive environment for all students.

Services Offered

CAPS offers a comprehensive suite of services tailored to meet your needs:

  • - Individual Counseling: One-on-one sessions with licensed therapists to explore personal, academic, or career-related concerns
  • - Group Counseling: Participate in a variety of support groups focused on themes such as stress management, anxiety, and identity
  • - Workshops and Outreach: Programs designed to enhance your coping skills, resilience, and personal growth
  • - Crisis Services: Immediate support for urgent mental health needs, available during and after business hours

To learn more about the CAPS services, please visit CAPS Services.

How to Access CAPS

Accessing CAPS services is straightforward and student-friendly:

  • - Initial Consultation: Schedule a brief screening appointment to discuss your needs with a counselor and determine the best course of action.
  • - Appointments: Appointments can be scheduled by calling our office or visiting in person.

FAQs and Additional Support

Q: Are CAPS services free?
A: Yes, all current students can access CAPS services at no cost

Q: Is my information confidential?
A: Absolutely. CAPS adheres to stringent confidentiality policies to ensure your privacy

Q: What if I need help outside regular hours?
A: CAPS provides after-hours crisis support via the U-M Crisis Line at (734) 936-5900

Taking care of your mental health is essential for your overall well-being and academic success. We encourage you to reach out, explore our resources, and connect with our community. Remember, you are not alone, and CAPS is here to support you every step of the way.
